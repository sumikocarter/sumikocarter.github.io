Hello Madwell!

My name is Sumiko Carter, and I am currently an Art Director & Designer at Threespot. As an adventure-seeker who walks the line between optimizing user experience and implementing interactive design, I believe I am an ideal candidate for your Designer role in Boulder, CO.

I have five years of experience as a multi-disciplinary designer, successfully bringing usability and awe-inspiring designs to a demanding national and international client list.

With degrees in both mathematics and studio art, and penchant for volunteer travel that has allowed me to immerse myself in diverse cultures, I bring a unique and global approach to my work that helps me to translate complex ideas into contemporary and visually arresting designs. I see endless possibilities on a blank canvas, and don’t need much design direction to create functional prototypes or to communicate visual narratives. I am equally comfortable with CSS/HTML, calculating abstract number theories and learning new mediums, like webflow.

I am inspired by your work, and it would be amazing to join and contribute to such a strong and talented team.

My Current Role

At Threespot, I lead and execute design for a range of clients, including Peace Corps, Natural Resources Defense Council, Planned Parenthood, and National Park Service teams. My current workday includes strategizing, evolving brands, producing high-quality mock-ups, and prototyping, all while working closely and collaboratively with clients. I joined Threespot in September 2011, after transitioning from my previous position at US News and World Report, where I helped launch US News Weekly for the iPad. But I got my real start in 2009 working as a designer for National Geographic International Publishing Group.

tl;dr

  • I am one of two Art Directors leading Threespot's design department and act as lead designer on most projects. I get the benefit of developing AND executing ideas for my clients’ brands in the digital space.

  • I love my Keep Cup and use it constantly (so much so, in fact, that I often forget to rinse it out before refilling). As for solid foods, I am a complete sucker for variety packs or yogurt/apple sauce sleeves.

  • My team designed and developed nine mobile-based apps within 9 weeks that encourage Latino and African-American teens to make informed decisions. The tools received extensive press coverage, including mentions in NBC’s TODAY show, Newsday, Think Progress, and USA TODAY.

  • I’ve dominated the company Halloween costume contest for three years running.

I am constantly searching for new ways to improve my skills and broaden my design experiences, through course work as well as artistic encounter. My favorite conference is Brooklyn Beta, hands down. Maybe I’ve seen you there?

A Brief Personal Journey

When I’m not manipulating pixels, I am a competitive volleyball player who loves nothing more than to spend my afternoons on the sand courts. As I’ve said, I love to travel. But I’m not interested in the usual: each time I hit the road, I seek the extraordinary. My journeys have included an education in tea ceremony along the pilgrimage trail in Shikoku, Japan, surfing and studying design in rural Huichol Mexico, snowboarding in the Alps, and galloping on horseback in the shadow of Egypt's Pyramids. I am fluent in French and conversational in Kiswahili.
